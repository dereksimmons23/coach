# Summer Training Program — Parent Email Template

> Use this as a starting point. Personalize the opening for each family.
> Brackets [ ] indicate spots to customize.

---

**Subject:** Summer basketball training — a few spots open

---

Hi [Parent Name],

Hope you and [Player Name] are doing well. [Optional personal note — how's the season going / heard they made varsity / good to see them at the tournament / etc.]

I'm putting together a small summer training program this June through August and wanted to reach out to families I've worked with before.

Here's the short version: 1-on-1 training once a week, plus a team competition session on a separate day with the other players in the program. Six players maximum. $150 per week.

The individual sessions are built around the same neuroplasticity-based curriculum I use with my current players — bilateral development, cognitive load training, weak-hand work, the stuff that actually changes how a player's brain processes the game. The team day is where it comes together: competitive reps, live situations, decision-making under pressure with other good players pushing each other.

This isn't a camp and it isn't AAU. It's focused development with a small group.

I've put together a page with more detail if you want to take a look:
https://coach.claudewill.io/summer/

If [Player Name] is interested — or if you have questions about how it would work with their summer schedule — just reply to this email and we can talk through it.

Thanks,
Coach D

---

## Notes

**Who gets this email:** 30-50 families of former players (ages 15-20). Derek has coached all of them. This is warm outreach, not cold.

**Personalization matters.** The opening line is where this stops being a mass email. A specific memory, a question about their season, a mention of their team — anything that says "I remember your kid."

**The link does the heavy lifting.** The email is the personal touch. The page answers the detail questions. Don't over-explain in the email.

**The ask is soft.** "Reply if interested" not "sign up now." These are families who know Derek. The conversation will happen naturally.

**Scheduling concern.** Many families will say "we're interested but July is AAU" or "they have a job." That's fine. Monthly commitment, flexible scheduling. Address this in the reply, not in the initial email.

**Don't send all at once.** If 15 people respond and there are 5 spots, that's a problem. Send in batches of 10-15. Fill spots, then send more if needed.
