The Program

This isn't a camp. It's not AAU. It's individual development with a small group of players who are serious about getting better.

Each player gets weekly 1-on-1 training tailored to where they are and where they want to go. On a separate day, everyone comes together for competitive team sessions — game situations, pressure reps, the stuff that only works when you've got other good players pushing you.

How It Works

1-on-1 Training

One hour per week. Individual skill work built on the BLAST curriculum — 157 drills across 16 categories, designed around neuroplasticity principles. We find your gaps, build your weak hand, and challenge your brain while developing your game.

1 hour / week • scheduled individually

Team Competition

One hour per week, separate day. All players together. Competitive drills, live situations, and controlled scrimmage work. This is where individual development meets real basketball — reading the game, making decisions, competing with purpose.

The Approach

Every session is built on neuroplasticity training — the idea that the brain changes when you challenge it, not when you repeat what's comfortable. We use bilateral development, cognitive load progressions, and constraint-based drills to build players who can think and adapt, not just execute patterns. Frustration isn't failure. It's the signal that your brain is rewiring.

The specific drills are basketball. The methodology is universal.
