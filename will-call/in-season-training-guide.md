# In-Season Basketball Training Packages
*Premium development packages for committed athletes (Ages 14-18)*

## MARKET POSITIONING

### Elite Youth Basketball Training Price Ranges (Ages 14-18)

**Individual Training Sessions**
- Entry Level: $50-75/hour (high school or college players, group settings)
- Mid-Tier: $75-125/hour (experienced coaches, individual attention)
- Premium: $125-250/hour (elite credentials, specialized methodology)
- Elite/Pro: $250-500+/hour (former pros, celebrity coaches, top-tier facilities)

**Monthly Package Pricing**

**Basic Packages ($200-400/month)**:
- 4x 60-minute sessions
- General skill development
- Limited or no remote support

**Premium Packages ($400-800/month)**:
- 4-8x 60-90 minute sessions
- Specialized skill development
- Text/email support between sessions
- Video analysis included

**Elite Packages ($800-1,500+/month)**:
- Unlimited or extensive session access
- 24/7 coach availability
- Advanced analytics and film study
- College recruitment support

## RECOMMENDED PACKAGE STRUCTURE

### "Elite Performance Package" - $500/month

**Training Sessions**
- 4x 90-minute sessions per month (one per week)
- Scheduled consistently (same day/time weekly when possible)
- Flexible rescheduling with 24-hour notice

**Text Support**
- Unlimited text communication Monday-Friday (9am-7pm)
- Response within 24 hours guaranteed
- Quick form checks via video submission
- Mental preparation support before games

**Film Review**
- Monthly video analysis (1-2 games or training sessions)
- Written breakdown with key observations
- Specific drill recommendations based on film
- Progress tracking over time

**Personalized Development Plan**
- Written assessment of current strengths and development areas
- Progressive skill development roadmap
- Monthly check-ins on progress
- Adjustment based on in-season performance

## BUSINESS MODEL SCALABILITY

### Capacity Calculation
- 4 sessions/month/athlete × 90 minutes = 6 hours training time
- + 5 hours/month admin (text support, film review, planning) = **11 hours/athlete/month**
- **Sustainable athlete load**: 7-8 athletes maximum at Elite tier
- **Monthly revenue potential**: $3,500-4,000 (8 athletes × $500)

### Growth Strategy

**Phase 1 (Months 1-3)**: Build Core Client Base
- Goal: 4-5 Elite Package athletes
- Monthly revenue: $2,000-2,500
- Focus: Delivering excellent results, documenting success

**Phase 2 (Months 4-6)**: Expand and Diversify
- Goal: 6-8 total athletes (mix of packages)
- Monthly revenue: $3,000-4,000
- Focus: Refining systems, building reputation

**Phase 3 (Months 7-12)**: Scale Through Leverage
- Goal: 10+ athletes through assistant coaches or group offerings
- Monthly revenue: $5,000-7,000
- Focus: Hire associate trainer, create group training options

## PARENT CONVERSATION FRAMEWORK

### Opening
*"[Athlete] had a breakthrough session recently. We addressed [specific challenge], and they really made significant progress. I want to build on that momentum through the season to make sure the progress sticks."*

### Present the Package
*"I've put together an in-season development package that would give [athlete] consistent training every week, plus my support between sessions. Here's what that would look like..."*

### Address Timing
*"Whether [athlete] makes the varsity team or plays club, they're the type of player who's going to keep working. This package is designed for athletes like them — committed to improvement regardless of the outcome."*

### Handle Objections

**If Cost Concern**: 
*"I totally understand that's a real investment. Let me show you what this breaks down to per week, and how it compares to other development options families typically consider..."*

**If Commitment Concern**:
*"It's month-to-month after the first month, so you're not locked into anything long-term. If [athlete's] schedule changes or you want to adjust, we can do that with 30 days notice."*

**If Results Concern**:
*"You saw what happened in our recent session — that's the type of breakthrough we can build on consistently. I'm documenting progress, so you'll have objective evidence of improvement every month."*

## RISK MITIGATION

### Common Challenges & Solutions

**Athlete doesn't show progress**
- Set realistic expectations upfront
- Document small wins
- Maintain parent communication
- Adjust training approach as needed

**Payment issues**
- Auto-pay systems
- Clear payment terms
- Professional invoicing
- Friendly reminder system

**Schedule conflicts**
- Consistent weekly time slot
- Clear rescheduling policy
- Makeup session system
- Maintain boundaries

**Parent dissatisfaction**
- Clear communication upfront
- Regular check-ins
- Manage expectations
- Provide evidence of progress
