1. Press Room
The press room is equipped with seating, workstations, and high-speed internet for journalists covering the game. It often includes a podium or stage for post-game interviews with players and coaches. Proximity to the court and locker rooms allows for easy access.

2. Will Call
The will call area is typically located in the main lobby or box office of the arena. This is where attendees pick up pre-purchased tickets or resolve ticketing issues before entering the venue2.

3. Hall of Fame
Many arenas feature a Hall of Fame area showcasing memorabilia, plaques, and displays celebrating the history and achievements of the team or notable players. This space is often located near the main entrance to engage fans as they arrive6.

4. Trophy Case
Trophy cases are prominently displayed within the arena, often near entrances or in high-traffic areas like concourses. They house championship trophies, awards, and other accolades to celebrate team success and inspire fans.

5. Film Room
The film room is a dedicated space for reviewing game footage. It typically includes large screens, projectors, and tiered seating for players and coaches to analyze performance and strategize6.

6. Trainer's Room
The trainer's room is equipped with treatment tables, medical supplies, ice baths, and rehabilitation equipment to address player injuries and recovery needs. It is often adjacent to locker rooms for convenience9.

7. Weight Room
The weight room includes state-of-the-art equipment such as free weights, resistance machines, cardio stations, and functional training tools. It serves as a hub for strength training and conditioning programs9.
