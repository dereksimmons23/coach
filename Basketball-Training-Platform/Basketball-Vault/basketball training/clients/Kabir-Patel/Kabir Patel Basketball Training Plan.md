### **Solo Training Challenges:**

1. **"Coach, I'm training alone and missing a lot of shots. How do I stay motivated?"**
	1. You're not training alone. You're training in front of a packed arena. Use your imagination. See the crowd. The teammates. The opponents. Hear my voice saying you don't miss two shots in a row! And if you are still missing shots, go back to form shooting for five minutes.
2. **"My left hand still feels weak on drives. What can I do without a defender?"**
	1. Keep practicing everything left handed. Brushing your teeth. Eating with a fork. Get 50 left shooting drill. You have to get to a point where you don't think you have a weak hand. Remember: Nobody could tell whether Chris Paul was right handed or left handed because he was equally good with both. That's you.
3. **"I'm getting tired during Get 50. Should I rest or push through?"**
	1. Get 50 is not an endurance drill. It's form shooting. A warm up. If you are getting tired from Get 50, we need more conditioning drills.
4. **"How do I practice the kill dribble decision-making when I'm alone?"**
	1. Use your imagination. See the defender. Count down the shot clock in your head. Down 2 with 15 seconds left. Up 5 with a minute to go. Tied in the first quarter. To make better decisions when alone, we have to use our imaginations and create the chaos to learn how to make choices. But also remember that slow is smooth and smooth is fast. That hesi isn't fooling anyone if you can't sell it as a jumper.
5. **"I can't get my step back balanced without a defender. Any tips?"**
	1. Rep it out. If you can't find your balance without a defender, you certainly won't be able to find it with one. And remember what I always said last season: Think with your feet first. For a step back, push off the leg to create space. Always practice at game speed, creating game shots
6. **"My float dribbles aren't covering enough space. What am I doing wrong?"**
	1. Likely not moving fast enough without the ball in your hands. Or worried about taking to many steps and traveling. This is like a defensive slide but on offense. Rep it out by using one dribble where you would stand for a free throw and using your far hand to touch the elbow while your other hand prepares to receive the ball, then reverse it, bouncing the ball once where you would shoot a free throw while sliding to use that same hand touch the opposite elbow while your right hand receives the ball. Get into a rhythm. Listen to a song that sets the pace. The good news: You're working on defensive slides and floating with the ball on offense at the same time.

### **Mental Game in India:**

7. **"I'm feeling frustrated training by myself. Miss having a coach."**
	1. Watch some videos on YouTube of Kobe. He didn't always have a coach in the gym telling him what to do. That's discipline. The good news is I'll be here when you get back. You will have all this solo development data we can review. And we can use WhatsApp for communication. Also, consider asking someone from your family to rebound for you. Even if they aren't a coach, they can still help you train, and it's quality time spent with family.
8. **"How do I know if I'm improving without you watching my form?"**
	1. Every few days, have someone from your family do a short video of you doing basketball training. Text it to me, and I'll give you some feedback. But if you're missing a lot of shots, you know enough about your shot to self diagnose. Are you holding your finish? For form shooting and free throws are you finishing on your toes? Is your arc flat? Get your palm under the ball and push up. Missing left or right? Make sure you are using your first two fingers to release the ball with spin, like a two-seam fastball. 
9. **"I'm tired but only halfway through my session. What would you tell me?"**
	1. Do you think the Top 10 incoming freshmen you will be competing against at high school tryouts get tired halfway through a session? Training tired is like preparing for the fourth quarter. Anyone can train halfway. And some players can get more done in half a session than most players can in a full session. Which player are you?
10. **"Missing shots is getting in my head. How do I reset mentally?"**
	1. Great shooters have memories like a goldfish: 10 seconds, tops. Did you forget how to shoot a basketball? Great shooters also always think the next shot is going in. Always. When in doubt, go back to form shooting, make a bunch, and that is your reset.

### **Technique Without Feedback:**

11. **"How do I know if my shooting form is right when training alone?"**
	1. We already answered this, I think, but how does the shot feel? Because when it feels right, even if it misses, it probably is right. if it doesn't feel right, go back to form shooting to make it feel right.
12. **"I think I'm rushing my shots. How do I slow down but stay smooth?"**
	1. Remember what I always say: How is the photographer going to get a cover worth shot of you hitting the game winner if you don't hold your finish and pose for the camera? Quick release? Yes. Rushing: No. I think it was John Wooden who said "be quick but not in a hurry."
13. **"My one-leg shooting in Get 50 feels off. What should I focus on?"**
	1. Balance. Control. This isn't a shooting drill. It's the basketball version of leg day in speed & strength.
14. **"How do I practice 'attacking feet' when there's no defender?"**
	1. Ideally, two cones, but even two wadded up pieces of paper will do. Move them around as if they are your opponent's shoes, or even use an old pair of sneakers! And then practice attacking that high foot, as well as the low foot. Very few players play perfectly straight up, but practice that too. And remember: Sometimes your best first move is a counter-move.
