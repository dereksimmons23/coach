# Tennis Ball & Basketball

**Category 8 of 16** | Combined coordination drills

---

## Overview

These drills combine a tennis ball in one hand with a basketball in the other. This forces divided attention and builds elite-level hand-eye coordination.

**Progression:**
- **Travel:** Cross underneath grab
- **Varsity:** Between grab, behind grab
- **Recruit:** Full pound between/behind grab sequences

---

## Drills

### Pound, Cross Underneath Grab - Alternating
**Levels:** Travel

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Sequence** | 4 dribble sequence, catching tennis ball twice |
| **Balance** | Feet wide, shoulders over knees |

**Modifications:**
- Pound basketball and tennis ball together, cross underneath catch, STOP, GATHER
- Pound basketball and tennis ball together, cross underneath, let tennis ball bounce once before catch

**Execution:**
1. 1st dribble: right hand basketball, left hand tennis ball
2. Bounce both balls straight down at the same time
3. 2nd dribble: right hand basketball cross underneath to left hand
4. Without letting tennis ball bounce a second time, grab it out of the air with right hand
5. 3rd dribble: left hand basketball, right hand tennis ball
6. Bounce both balls straight down at the same time
7. 4th dribble: left hand basketball cross underneath to right hand
8. Without letting tennis ball bounce a second time, grab it out of the air with left hand

### Pound, Between/Behind Grab, Pound Cross - Alternating

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Sequence** | 10 dribble sequence, catching tennis ball twice |
| **Balance** | Feet wide, shoulders over knees |

**Modifications:** None

**Execution:**
1. 1st dribble: right hand basketball, left hand tennis ball
2. Bounce both balls straight down at the same time
3. 2nd dribble: right hand basketball between legs to left hand
4. 3rd dribble: left hand behind back
5. Without letting tennis ball bounce a second time, grab it with left hand
6. 4th dribble: right hand basketball and left hand tennis ball together, pound cross underneath
7. Repeat sequence on other side (dribbles 5-10)

## Coaching Notes

**Why Both Balls:**
- Forces divided attention
- Builds reaction speed
- Develops ambidexterity
- Simulates game chaos

**Common Errors:**
- Watching the tennis ball (trust your hands)
- Letting tennis ball bounce twice (speed up the catch)
- Not staying low

**Cues:**
- "Grab it out of the air"
- "Don't let it bounce twice"
- "Feet wide, shoulders over knees"
- "Trust your hands"
