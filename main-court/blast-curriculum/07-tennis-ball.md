# Tennis Ball

**Category 7 of 16** | Coordination drills using tennis ball only

---

## Overview

Tennis ball drills develop hand-eye coordination, reaction time, and ambidexterity. The smaller ball forces precision and focus.

**Progression:**
- **Travel:** Behind back patterns, 2-ball inside out
- **Varsity:** Pound between patterns, inside out between cross

---

## Drills

### Pound Behind - Alternating
**Levels:** Travel

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Sequence** | 4 dribble sequence |
| **Balance** | Feet wide, shoulders over knees |

**Modifications:**
- 6 dribble sequence
- 8 dribble sequence

**Execution:**
1. 1st dribble: right hand pound straight down
2. 2nd dribble: right hand behind back
3. 3rd dribble: left hand pound straight down
4. 4th dribble: left hand behind back
5. Repeat sequence

---

### 2 Balls, Inside Out Dribble Alternating
**Levels:** Travel

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Sequence** | 2 dribble sequence |
| **Balance** | Feet wide, shoulders over knees |

**Modifications:**
- Off the wall: keep bicep close to rib cage, one dribble push off wall continuous, hand placement outside and inside

**Execution:**
1. 1st dribble: right hand pound straight down
2. 2nd dribble: right hand between legs
3. 3rd dribble: left hand crossover back to right hand
4. Repeat sequence

## Coaching Notes

**Why Tennis Ball:**
- Smaller ball requires more precision
- Builds hand-eye coordination
- Forces focus on fundamentals
- Transfers to basketball control

**Common Errors:**
- Using palm instead of fingertips
- Looking at the ball (keep head up)
- Standing up (stay low)

**Cues:**
- "Feet wide, shoulders over knees"
- "Fingertips, not palm"
- "Keep your head up"
