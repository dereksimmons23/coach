## Game Management Insights

### Consecutive Period Management

**The 3-period rule for Top 4 players:**
- Never play 3+ consecutive periods if avoidable
- Fatigue reduces decision-making quality
- Risk of injury increases
- Leadership diluted when exhausted

**How to avoid:**
- Use Unit A/B default (automatically alternates)
- Monitor drag-and-drop adjustments (tool warns)
- Accept occasional 2 consecutive (yellow) if strategic
- Never accept 3 consecutive (red) for Top 4 (orange pulse)

**When to break the rule:**
- Tournament finals (championship at stake)
- Close game in final periods (trust your leaders)
- Opponent goes small/big (matchup adjustment)
- Foul trouble forces hand (no choice)

### Overtime Strategy

**10-Player OT Distribution:**
- Each player gets 1 OT period
- OT1: Dylan, Caleb, Ben, Kabir, John (Top 4 + Ben)
- OT2: Charlie, Tommy, Michael, Teegan, Dominic (remaining 5)

**9-Player OT Distribution:**
- Top 4 + Charlie play both OTs (5 players)
- Ben, Michael, Dominic, John play 5 regular periods, no OT

**Coaching logic:**
- Trust proven performers in high-pressure moments
- Rest players who logged heavy regular period minutes
- Match intensity with experience
- Don't burn out Top 4 before OT even starts

### Substitution Philosophy

**"Everyone plays, but not everyone plays the same"**

**WAA rule compliance:**
- Equal number of periods per player (based on roster size)
- But NOT equal positions or opponents
- Strategic deployment within equal time framework

**How Coach D uses this:**
- Tommy never guards the best perimeter player (positions 2-3 only)
- Teegan anchors paint defense (position 5 primary)
- Ben/Michael get easier defensive matchups (support Top 4)
- Kabir/Caleb guard toughest assignments (Top 4 versatility)

**Key insight:** Equal playing time â‰  equal responsibility. Teach all, trust strategically.

## Decision-Making Framework

### How Coach D Makes Lineup Decisions

**Hierarchy of considerations:**

**1. WAA Compliance (Non-negotiable)**
- Equal playing time per roster size
- No technical fouls
- Official rules followed exactly

**2. Player Safety (Non-negotiable)**
- Avoid 3+ consecutive periods (fatigue/injury)
- Top 4 get strategic rest
- Monitor energy levels in-game

**3. Strategic Matchups (Flexible)**
- Position players for success
- Exploit opponent weaknesses
- Protect defensive liabilities

**4. Player Development (Long-term)**
- Give players different experiences
- Test versatility in live games
- Build confidence through success

**5. Winning (Important, not paramount)**
- Trust Top 4 in close games
- Deploy best lineups in critical moments
- Balance winning today with developing for tomorrow

### When Rules Conflict

**Example:** Top 4 player needs to play 3 consecutive periods to satisfy WAA equal time rule.

**Coach D's approach:**
1. Exhaust all alternatives (drag-and-drop different combos)
2. Accept if absolutely necessary (flag in validation)
3. Monitor in-game (sub earlier if fatigued)
4. Debrief after game (how to avoid next time)

**Principle:** Rules serve development, not vice versa. If rule doesn't serve kids, question the rule (respectfully).
